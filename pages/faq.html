Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about life at O'Conor Home.

General Questions

O'Conor Home offers residential care, hospital-level care, and specialised dementia care. Our team is equipped to support residents through all stages of ageing, with a focus on dignity, comfort, and remaining connected to the Westport community.

You can get in touch with our team via the contact page, by phone, or by email. We welcome families to visit at any time and encourage a tour before making any decisions. Please contact us to arrange a time that suits you.

Our fees cover accommodation, all meals, personal care, nursing support, and activities. We are transparent about any additional costs upfront. Please contact us for a full breakdown of our current fee structure.

Yes — we actively encourage residents to bring familiar items from home. Personal photographs, treasured objects, and even some pieces of furniture help make the space feel truly theirs. Our team will work with you to personalise each room.

Family and friends are welcome to visit at any time. We believe staying connected to loved ones is essential to wellbeing and we have no restrictions on visiting hours. Our team is always happy to assist with arrangements for special occasions.

We offer a varied programme of activities including social gatherings, arts and crafts, music, gentle exercise, outings, and community events. Our activities are designed around the interests and abilities of our residents. Check our events page for upcoming activities.

Dementia Care Questions

We understand that choosing dementia care for a loved one is one of the most significant decisions a family can make. These questions and answers are here to help guide you.

Yes. O'Conor Home provides specialised dementia and memory care as part of our hospital-level services. Our staff are trained specifically in dementia care and we have purpose-designed spaces to support residents living with Alzheimer's and other forms of dementia.

All staff members receive specialised training in dementia care, including how dementia affects cognition, communication, and emotion. Staff are trained to manage behaviours such as distress, confusion, wandering, and agitation with compassion and evidence-based techniques. Ongoing training ensures our team stays current with best practices in memory care.

We maintain a high staff-to-resident ratio to ensure each resident receives attentive, personalised care throughout the day and night. Please contact us directly for our current staffing ratios, which we are happy to discuss openly with families.

Our environment is designed with dementia residents in mind. This includes brighter lighting to reduce the risk of falls and anxiety, clear signage with pictures and colour coding, dementia-friendly furniture layouts, quiet spaces for reflection, and secure outdoor areas. We aim to create a calm, homelike environment that reduces confusion and supports independence.

Resident safety is our highest priority. We have secure areas designed to allow safe movement and independence while preventing wandering beyond safe boundaries. Staff monitor residents around the clock and care plans are updated in collaboration with families as needs change. Our approach balances safety with dignity — residents are never made to feel restricted.

We take time to build a detailed picture of each new resident — their life story, preferences, routines, and what brings them comfort. We welcome memory books, photographs, and stories from family members. This information helps our team connect meaningfully with residents even as memory changes over time. We encourage families to share as much as they can before and after admission.

Challenging behaviours in dementia are understood as a form of communication. Our team uses non-medication-based approaches as a first response — including redirection, personalised activities, environmental adjustments, and calm, familiar communication. Where behaviours persist, our medical team assesses for underlying causes such as pain, anxiety, or medication reactions and adapts care plans accordingly.

Activities for residents with dementia are tailored to their current abilities and interests. Research shows that arts, music, gentle exercise, and social interaction can boost mood and slow the progression of dementia. We offer person-centred programming designed to provide purpose, enjoyment, and connection — both in group settings and one-on-one.

Where possible, yes. Involving the person with dementia in care decisions honours their autonomy and can ease the transition. Our team is experienced in supporting these conversations with sensitivity. When cognitive decline makes direct involvement difficult, we work closely with families to ensure decisions reflect the values and wishes of the person in our care.
